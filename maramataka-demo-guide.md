# Maramataka Demo — Your Three-Part Package

-----

## 1. The App (maramataka-demo.html)

This is your polished demo file. It includes:

- **Splash screen** — animated moon, “Tīmata · Begin” button, and the Wawata credit
- **Clock tab** — live clock, accurate moon phase, Māori phase name, Wawata reflection
- **Calendar tab** — monthly grid with equal cells, phase names, appointments
- **About tab** — app description, features list, and a Wawata/Dr Elder section
- Full appointment system with colour coding saved to the device

## 2. How to Get a Shareable Link (Free — 10 minutes)

This puts your app on a real URL anyone can open, including Dr Elder.

### Step-by-step: GitHub Pages

1. **Create a free account** at github.com
1. **Create a new repository**
- Click the **+** button → New repository
- Name it: `maramataka`
- Set it to **Public**
- Tick “Add a README file”
- Click **Create repository**
1. **Upload your file**
- In your new repository, click **Add file** → **Upload files**
- Upload `maramataka-demo.html`
- **Important:** Rename it to `index.html` before uploading
  (or rename it after by clicking the file → Edit → rename)
- Click **Commit changes**
1. **Turn on GitHub Pages**
- Go to **Settings** (top menu of your repository)
- Scroll down to **Pages** (left sidebar)
- Under “Branch”, select `main` and click **Save**
- Wait 2–3 minutes
1. **Your link will be:**
   `https://YOUR-USERNAME.github.io/maramataka`
   
   That’s the link you send Dr Elder. She can open it on any phone or computer.

### Tips for recording

- Use your actual phone — it looks more authentic than a simulator
- Record in a quiet spot, no background noise
- You can do multiple takes — just use the best one
- Trim the start and end in the Photos app
- Send the video via email or as an Instagram DM to @drhinemoa
